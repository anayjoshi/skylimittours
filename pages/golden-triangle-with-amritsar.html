Golden Triangle with Amritsar

Destinations Covered in Tour

(7 Nights / 8 Days).

Delhi (2 nights) > Amritsar (1 night) > Delhi (1 night) > Agra (1 night) > Jaipur (2 night)

  • Delhi: The Capital of India Amritsar
  • Agra: Famous for TajMahal
  • Jaipur: The Capital of Rajasthan known as Pink City

Detailed Itinerary

Day-01: Delhi. On arrival in Delhi, you will be met and transferred by our representative to your hotel. Overnight: New Delhi

Day-02: Delhi. After breakfast, full day city tour of old & modern capital of India. Visit Jama Masjid, the largest mosque in India. Built by Shah Jahan in the seventeenth century, Red Fort, which was also built by Shah Jehan. This massive fort was built from 1638 to 1648, enjoy a rickshaw ride, and visit the Chandni Chowk and Rajghat - the cremation site of Mahatma Gandhi. Witness the India Gate, the Presidents House & the Parliament. Visit Humayun's Tomb: built in mid 16th century this is an early example of Mughal architecture; Qutab Minar. On the way our team will brief you about the various interesting facts related to the spots you visit and also help you purchase entry tickets. Overnight: New Delhi

Day-03: Delhi to Amritsar (Flight). Morning, transfer to the Airport to board flight for Amritsar. Arrive Amritsar & our representative will receive you at the airport. Visit the Jallianwala Bagh-the historic site where hundreds of innocent Indian men, women an children were massacred by British General Michael Dyer on April 13, 1919. A memoral and Garden have been created here as a national monument. Evening - The Wagah border often called the "Berlin wall of Asia” is a ceremonial border on India–Pakistan Border, where each evening, there is a retreat ceremony called 'lowering of the flags. It is a site that you wouldn’t want to miss and we will ensure that you can get the best glimpses. with the Border Security Force on the Indian Side and the Sutlej Rangers on the Pakistan side putting up a well coordinated and spectacular display. The sound from the Bugles blown together from both sides paints past on the canvas when India and Pakistan were one Overnight: Amritsar

Day-04: Amritsar – New Delhi (477 Kms, 7-8 hrs Drive). After breakfast, city tour of Amritsar - Golden Temple-the holy scripture is brought in a vivid procession from the Akal Takhat (the highest temporal seat of the Sikhs) in the morning to the temple to be returned at night. Singing is central to Sikh worship & is a feast to the souls when hymns from the holy scripture are recited Kirtan and the waves of the classical ragas, a sublime blend of voice & music. Afternoon, transfer to the railway station to board train for Delhi. Arrive Delhi railway station, transfer to your hotel. Overnight: New Delhi

Day-05: New Delhi – Agra (203 Kms, 3-4 hrs Drive). After Breakfast, leave for Agra – Visit Sikandra Enroute- the tomb of the third mughal ruler Akbar (The first emperor to plan his own tomb). Visit Red fort, which was built by Emperor Akbar in 1565, and additions were made up until the time of his grandson, Shah Jahan. It contains the hall of public and private audiences amongst many other beautiful structures. While you can hire guides at these places, the accompanying driver will surely help you with brief details and ticketing whenever you visit the monuments. In the Evening visit the Taj Mahal to the see the beautiful sight of the sunset, as a famous indian poet Rabindranath tagore descried it – "a teardrop on the cheek of time" it is the most beautiful expression of love on this planet. Overnight: Agra

Day-06: Agra – Jaipur (232 Kms, 3-4 hrs Drive). After breakfast, leave for Jaipur , The capital city of the state of Rajasthan also know as “Pink City” owes its name, its foundation and planning to the great warrior astronomer Maharaja Jai Singh II. Visit en route Fatehpur Sikri en route. Fatehpur Sikri is a walled town built by the third mughal Emperor Akbar in 1569 AD as a ceremonial capital. But unfortunately it was abandoned due to scarcity of water. Upon arrival in Jaipur a representative assists you to transfer to the hotel.. Evening, visit the city palace and the observatory built by the great ruler Maharaja Sawai Jai singh Ji II. Overnight: Jaipur

Day-07: Jaipur. We are in the land of the Maharajas and we start our day with the visit to the Amber fort in the outskirt of Jaipur overlooking wonderful view of the city. Enjoy royal elephant ride to ascend the fort complex. Later visit the Jagmandir or the Hall of victory glitterning with mirrors, also see the Nahargarh fort. Evening, free for independent activities like shopping in the markets for Jaipur gemstones or clothes Dinner at Chokhi dani (an ethnic village resort) where you will have an idea of the village life of Rajasthan here you will also enjoy various traditional Rajasthani dances, puppet show and various other interesting activities. Overnight: Jaipur

Day-08: Jaipur – Delhi (258 Kms, 4-5 hrs Drive). This morning start you drive to New Delhi (Approx 5 Hrs) to catch your flight for next Destination/Home. Golden Triangle with Amritsar Photo Gallery
